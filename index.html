I hold a Ph.D. in Machine Learning applied to Computational Biology from the University of Navarra. My doctoral research focused on graph learning—particularly knowledge graphs and graph neural networks—as well as a broad spectrum of representation learning techniques, ranging from variational autoencoders to causal representation frameworks. I specialized in leveraging multi-omics datasets, including bulk RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq (scRNA-seq), spatial transcriptomics, and Perturb-seq. As part of my Ph.D., I completed a research stay at the Center for Data Science at New York University as a Fulbright Excellence Fellow.

Currently, I am focused on the design and implementation of machine learning models for MFT (medium-frequency trading) strategies on cryptocurrency derivatives. My work integrates real-time prediction, strategy optimization, and the deployment of high-performance learning systems for market modeling.

I have published in top machine learning conferences and journals, including ICLR 2025 and Nature Machine Intelligence. My full publication record is available on my Google Scholar profile.

I’m also an active participant in the machine learning community and a proud Kaggle Competitions Expert. I enjoy working on competitive data science problems, and I’m passionate about applying AI to interdisciplinary challenges in biology, healthcare, and finance.
